What are LEDs?
An LED, short for light emitting diode, is an electronic component that emits light when
current flows through it. LEDs come in a variety of colours, and are widely used in many
fields and applications.

What is Ohm's Law?
Ohm's Law states that the voltage across a conductor between two points is equal to the
current through the conductor multiplied by the conductor's resistance, thus, Ohm's Law
can be expressed as V = I * R.
