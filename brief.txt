What are LEDs?
An LED, short for light emitting diode, is an electronic component that emits light when
current flows through it. LEDs come in a variety of colours, and are widely used in many
fields and applications.

What is Ohm's Law?
Ohm's Law states that the voltage across a conductor between two points is equal to the
current through the conductor multiplied by the conductor's resistance, thus, Ohm's Law
can be expressed as V = I * R.

How can we calculate the appropriate resistor for an LED?
Every circuit will have one voltage source that supplies a voltage of either 3 V or 5 V.
Without a resistor current will be extremely high since I = V / R, thus a resistor must
be added to the circuit to reduce current and prevent the LED from overheating. Your goal
is to determine an appropriate resistance for the resistor that will prevent the LED from
overheating, but still allow the LED to light up.
